This might not quite involve knocking colorful balls around using a long stick, but this carrom event comes close. Standard carrom rules apply, but you must play from where the striker is. All about placement, this game requires a keen eye for angles and speed.


Game Rules

Top

  • All the college teams willing to participate in the event should register online.
  • Maximum teams allowed for registration would be 24.
  • Any number of teams of a college can register for this event.
  • Registrations will be confirmed based on first come first serve basis. Each college team should have a valid college ID proof for participation.
  • The aim of the game is to put one's maximum carrommen before one's opponent puts his/hers within the time constraint. The last position of the striker will always be the starting location for the next shot to be played by the next player (as in Pool)
  • The baseline will not be used as a benchmark for hitting one's coin (except in the beginning shot, when the shot has to be hit from the baseline only)
  • The queen can be pocketed and then "covered" only after pocketing 4 coins of one's own carrommen.
  • Dragging the striker is not allowed. The striker has to be hit as done in conventional carrom game.
  • Any of the hands can be used for hitting the striker during a shot.
  • A player has to remain seated while playing a shot from the striker (even if the striker is distant from him).
  • However body parts can cross the diagonal while playing a shot.
  • Points for coins will have same weightage as in conventional form of game (3 for queen and 1 for a white/black carrom coin)
  • The points secured by a team depends on the number of coins pocketed during the board.
  • The time limit for each board would be 10 min. after which the difference of points secured by teams in that board will be noted and a new board will be started.
  • Case 1: If Team A has pocketed 5 White carrommen and Team B has pocketed 4 Black carrommen and Queen has not been pocketed, Team A will get 5-4 = 1 point for that board and Team B will not get any point.
  • Case 2: If Team A has pocketed Queen also along with 5 White coins whereas Team B has pocketed 4 Black coins, then Team A will get (5-4)+3 = 4 points for that board and Team B will not get any point.
  • Case 3: If Team A has pocketed 5 White coins and Team B has pocketed 4 Black coins along with Queen, then Team B will get (4+3) - 5 = 2 points for the board and Team A will not get any point.
  • If a player commits a foul by pocketing a striker without any coin, then a foul will be placed by opponent and the next player will get a chance to hit the striker anywhere from the baseline.
  • If a player commits a foul by pocketing a striker along with one or more of his/her own coins, then those coins along with one foul will be placed by the opponent and the same player will get a chance to hit the striker anywhere from his baseline.


Event Conduction

Top

  • The event will only be a Doubles Event. (Boys Doubles, Girls Doubles and Mixed doubles are allowed)
  • The teams will be divided into pools. There will be 4 boards conducted between two teams(each of 10 min. duration as mentioned above).
  • The sum of points secured till 4 boards will be considered for declaring the winner. In the case of a tie at the end of 4th board, the tie will be broken by playing an extra board (not a complete match) until the tie is broken.
  • After the final results, top 3 positions will be rewarded.
  • All the particpating teams have to bring their own Striker for the Carrom Event. Rest of the accessories will be made available during the event.
  • Any queries or discrepancies regarding the rules will be sorted out by the Event Manager and/or the Referee present.
  • Any team or player found guilty of misconduct or violating the above rules may be disqualified.
  • Mobile phones should be turned off inside the carom hall.
  • Keep checking this page for any latest update for the event.

Spree

The Annual Sports Festival of the BITS Pilani, K.K. Birla Goa Campus.

In a quest to unravel the spirit of sporting competition and promote the fortitude of true sportsmanship, SPREE offers a plethora of fiercely contested events. To add to the sporting exhilaration, a special event is organized alongside each major sports event. SPREE 2011 saw Cricket accompanied by Gully Cricket, Football with Futsal, Basketball with 3BA, Volleyball with 3vs3 and Kick Volleyball, Lawn Tennis with Mini court Tennis, Table Tennis with Doubles, Carrom with C-Pool, Badminton with Mixed Doubles and Chess with anti-Chess. A diverse mix of off-beat events such as Pool, Kabaddi, Kho-Kho, and Go-Karting added to the fervor. SPREE 2012 shall also unveil the Goa Marathon this year round.

SPREE 2011 witnessed as many as 1000 participants from colleges all over the country fighting it out for glory including colleges from as far as Kashmir. And this time, SPREE 2012 is all set to take the country by storm. And as if the heart pounding thrills and spills of the on-field action just aren't enough, SPREE presents an array of Special Nights featuring mesmerizing performances from renowned artists from all across the globe. Karaoke and DJ nites add to the enthrallment of the fest. In addition to this, crowds were spellbound by adrenalin pumping performances by African acrobats, jugglers and fire performers as well.

SPREE 2012 shall witness the best of sporting talent battle it out to clinch the crown of glory. The campus, equipped with state of the art sporting facilities of Badminton courts, Cricket ground, Football field, Futsal arena and Volleyball courts is all set to set the dais of sporting extravaganza ablaze, this March 2012. With an exciting line-up of sporting clashes coupled with additional special events, 'SPREE 2012 – Battle for Survival' promises to take the notch of sheer sporting talent a notch higher.
