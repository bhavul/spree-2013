A new game to engage all the football fanatics and jugglers. It is a modification of the game, Volleyball with a touch of football. The game is an easier version of the game called �Sepak Takraw.� Teams of 3 play a foot-volley match with the intent of winning the game by returning the football to the other side of the net by juggling it with any part of their body (like feet, head , thighs, shoulders chest , back) except their arms. One bounce is allowed every time the ball enters your side of the court. This makes it easier to control and gives people who are not expert freestylists a fair chance to play the game. A team can juggle the ball any number of times as long as only one bounce is taken.


  • The net should be around 6.5 feet high. (Badminton court dimensions would be perfect for the game.)
  • The game will be played with a normal football (size 5).
  • 3 people per team (or can have 4 with a rolling substitute).
  • The ball can be juggled any no. of times on a side as long as maximum one bounce is taken.
  • If ball is dropped more than once than that point will be lost.
  • The ball has to be served with one person lofting the ball high and the other heading this ball to the other side.
  • Ball hitting the net and crossing it during service will be re-served.
  • Ball dropping completely outside the line will be considered out and point will be lost. Referee�s decision will be final.
  • Each point will result in one point to the winner and serve will change to the point winner.
  • 2 sets will be played in each game. Teams will switch sides. Each set will be 11 points. 11th point will be the game point.
  • At 10-10 score, deuce-advantage will be played out for the set winner.
  • If both teams win a set each then point difference will be considered. If they are also the same then a third decider set will be played.
  • The ball can be juggled anywhere even outside the court as long as it doesn�t bounce there. But the ball has to be returned over the net.
  • No part of the body should come in contact with the net when the game is on (like volley ball).
  • An exhibition match will be played to explain the game and to clear up the other minor details.
  • Referee�s decision will be one and final.

Spree

The Annual Sports Festival of the BITS Pilani, K.K. Birla Goa Campus.

In a quest to unravel the spirit of sporting competition and promote the fortitude of true sportsmanship, SPREE offers a plethora of fiercely contested events. To add to the sporting exhilaration, a special event is organized alongside each major sports event. SPREE 2011 saw Cricket accompanied by Gully Cricket, Football with Futsal, Basketball with 3BA, Volleyball with 3vs3 and Kick Volleyball, Lawn Tennis with Mini court Tennis, Table Tennis with Doubles, Carrom with C-Pool, Badminton with Mixed Doubles and Chess with anti-Chess. A diverse mix of off-beat events such as Pool, Kabaddi, Kho-Kho, and Go-Karting added to the fervor. SPREE 2012 shall also unveil the Goa Marathon this year round.

SPREE 2011 witnessed as many as 1000 participants from colleges all over the country fighting it out for glory including colleges from as far as Kashmir. And this time, SPREE 2012 is all set to take the country by storm. And as if the heart pounding thrills and spills of the on-field action just aren't enough, SPREE presents an array of Special Nights featuring mesmerizing performances from renowned artists from all across the globe. Karaoke and DJ nites add to the enthrallment of the fest. In addition to this, crowds were spellbound by adrenalin pumping performances by African acrobats, jugglers and fire performers as well.

SPREE 2012 shall witness the best of sporting talent battle it out to clinch the crown of glory. The campus, equipped with state of the art sporting facilities of Badminton courts, Cricket ground, Football field, Futsal arena and Volleyball courts is all set to set the dais of sporting extravaganza ablaze, this March 2012. With an exciting line-up of sporting clashes coupled with additional special events, 'SPREE 2012 – Battle for Survival' promises to take the notch of sheer sporting talent a notch higher.
